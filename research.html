Distinguished Doctoral Degree

Highly recognized scientists may apply to EMAAS for the Distinguished Doctoral Degree.

To qualify for the EMAAS Distinguished Doctoral Degree, a scientist must have a university PhD degree and more than 100 scientific publications, must have conducted research, contributed in solving scientific problems in their specific area, and supervised PhD students.

The application to EMAAS for this degree must be supported by a Curriculum Vitae, a memorandum on scientific research activities and contributions in solving scientific problems, and a list of publications. All the above must be sent in the form of electronic files to the President of the Academy (kervalpt@yahoo.com) along with the sum of Euros 500 to cover administrative and paperwork expenses.
